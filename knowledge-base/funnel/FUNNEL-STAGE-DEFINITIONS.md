# KB-05: Funnel Stage Definitions — Acefone

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last updated:** [DATE]  
**Used by:** Content Writer Agent, SEO Agent  
**Purpose:** Defines TOFU, MOFU, and BOFU for Acefone — what each stage means, who the reader is, what they need, and how content must be calibrated.

---

## Why funnel stage matters

Every piece of content must be written for a specific reader at a specific moment in their journey. A TOFU reader hasn't identified their problem clearly yet. A BOFU reader is comparing vendors. Content written for the wrong stage wastes the reader's time and produces zero business impact.

**Rule:** No content brief is valid without a declared funnel stage. The Content Writer Agent must reject any brief that omits this field.

---

## TOFU — Top of Funnel

### Who the reader is
Someone who has recognised a symptom of a problem but hasn't defined it yet. They are researching broadly, often using discovery keywords. They are not thinking about vendors.

**Typical job title:** IT Manager, Operations Lead, SMB owner  
**Mindset:** "I think we have a comms problem. Let me understand the space."  
**Trust level with Acefone:** Zero — this may be their first encounter.

### What they need
Education, not sales. They need a framework for thinking about their problem. They need to feel smarter after reading — not sold to.

### Content types for TOFU
- SEO explainer blogs (what is X, how does X work)
- Industry trend pieces
- Problem-framing articles
- Listicles that map a pain point
- Thought leadership on industry shifts

### Tone and structure rules
- No product mentions in the first half of the piece
- No pricing, no demos, no "book a call"
- Jargon-free — write as if the reader is smart but new to this category
- Teach before selling — earn trust through depth
- CTA: soft — "read more", "explore the guide", newsletter sign-up

### SEO intent signals (TOFU keywords)
- "What is [X]"
- "How does [X] work"
- "Types of [X]"
- "Best way to [achieve outcome]"
- "Problems with [current approach]"

### Word count range: 1,200–2,500 words

---

## MOFU — Middle of Funnel

### Who the reader is
Someone who has defined their problem and is now actively researching solutions. They are comparing approaches, evaluating categories, and building a shortlist. They know Acefone exists but haven't committed.

**Typical job title:** Head of IT, Head of Customer Experience, Digital Transformation lead  
**Mindset:** "I know I need a cloud comms platform. I'm figuring out which type and which vendor."  
**Trust level with Acefone:** Aware — may have read TOFU content or seen an ad.

### What they need
Comparison, proof, and clarity. They need help making a decision. They want to see that Acefone understands their specific problem — not a generic solution.

### Content types for MOFU
- Comparison pieces (us vs. category, not us vs. named competitors publicly)
- Use case deep-dives
- How-to guides tied to their role or industry
- Case study summaries
- Feature explanation content
- ROI and value calculators

### Tone and structure rules
- Acefone can be named — but lead with the problem and solution, not the product
- Specific is better than general — use numbers, outcomes, real scenarios
- Address objections directly in the content
- CTA: medium friction — "download the guide", "see a demo", "compare plans"
- Evidence required — case studies, stats, product specifics

### SEO intent signals (MOFU keywords)
- "Best [X] for [industry/role]"
- "[X] vs [Y]"
- "How to choose [X]"
- "Benefits of [X]"
- "[Use case] solution"

### Word count range: 1,500–3,000 words

---

## BOFU — Bottom of Funnel

### Who the reader is
Someone who has shortlisted Acefone (among others) and is close to a buying decision. They need final reassurance. Objections are specific. Stakes feel high.

**Typical job title:** CTO, IT Director, Procurement lead  
**Mindset:** "I'm almost ready to sign. I need to know Acefone is the right call and that I won't regret it."  
**Trust level with Acefone:** High — they've likely spoken to sales or had a demo.

### What they need
Proof, specificity, and confidence. This is not the place for education. They need to see exactly how Acefone solves their problem, what implementation looks like, and what the risk of choosing Acefone is (and why that risk is low).

### Content types for BOFU
- Full case studies with ROI metrics
- Product deep-dive pages
- Integration and implementation guides
- Security, compliance, and SLA documentation
- Pricing and plan comparison
- Onboarding and support explainers

### Tone and structure rules
- Product-forward — lead with specific features and outcomes
- Numbers everywhere — cost savings, uptime %, deployment time
- Risk reversal — address the "what if it doesn't work" fear
- CTA: high commitment — "book a call", "start your trial", "speak to sales"
- No fluff — every sentence must move the reader toward a decision

### SEO intent signals (BOFU keywords)
- "[Brand name] review"
- "[Brand name] pricing"
- "[Brand name] vs [competitor]"
- "[Product] for [specific industry]"
- "Buy [X]", "get [X]", "[X] trial"

### Word count range: 800–2,000 words (depth over length)

## Quick reference card (for content brief completion)

| | TOFU | MOFU | BOFU |
|--|------|------|------|
| **Reader knows the problem?** | Partially | Yes | Fully |
| **Knows Acefone?** | No | Maybe | Yes |
| **Primary goal** | Educate | Evaluate | Decide |
| **Acefone mentions** | None or 1 at end | Yes, but solution-led | Product-forward |
| **CTA style** | Soft | Medium | High commitment |
| **Proof required** | Light | Moderate | Heavy |
| **Tone** | Friendly expert | Knowledgeable peer | Trusted partner |

---

## Rules that apply at all funnel stages

1. **No hallucinated links.** If you can't verify a source URL, do not include it.
2. **No competitor names in published content** unless the piece is explicitly a comparison article approved by the Head of Content.
3. **Every piece of content must end with exactly one CTA.** Not zero, not two — one.
4. **The headline must match the funnel stage.** A TOFU headline should not sound like an ad.
