## Standard slide structures

The Strategy Agent uses these as the default skeleton for each deck type. It fills in content — it does not change the structure unless the brief requires it.

## Template 1 — Executive Presentation (internal or client-facing)

**Use when:** Board updates, QBRs, senior stakeholder briefings, strategy sessions  
**Slide count:** 8–12  
**Tone:** Authoritative, concise, data-led

| Slide # | Title | Purpose | Content guidance |
|---------|-------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | [Company/project name] | Cover | Title + presenter + date only |
| 2 | Situation | Set context | 2–3 bullets: where we are now |
| 3 | The challenge | Define the problem | Be specific — the sharper the problem, the clearer the solution |
| 4 | The opportunity | Reframe the problem as a business opportunity | Size it where possible |
| 5 | Our approach | How we're responding | 3 strategic moves or priorities |
| 6 | Evidence / proof | Validate the approach | Data, case study, benchmark |
| 7 | Plan | What happens next | Timeline with owners and milestones |
| 8 | What we need | Ask or decision required | One clear ask |
| 9 | Appendix (optional) | Supporting detail | Charts, full data, methodology |

**Design notes:** Dark background for cover and closing slide. Light for content slides. One chart or visual per slide. No more than 5 bullet points per slide — if there are more, split into two slides.

## Template 2 — Sales Enablement Deck

**Use when:** Sales pitch, demo follow-up, product walkthrough  
**Slide count:** 10–15  
**Tone:** Confident, customer-focused, proof-heavy

| Slide # | Title | Purpose | Content guidance |
|---------|-------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | Cover | First impression | Company name, prospect name, date |
| 2 | Your situation | Show you've done homework | Restate the prospect's problem in their language |
| 3 | What's at stake | Quantify the cost of inaction | Business impact of the current situation |
| 4 | How we solve it | Solution overview | Lead with the outcome, not the feature |
| 5 | Feature 1 | Key capability | Feature → what it means for them → proof |
| 6 | Feature 2 | Key capability | Same structure |
| 7 | Feature 3 | Key capability | Same structure |
| 8 | Customers like you | Social proof | Named case study or anonymised outcome |
| 9 | Why Acefone | Differentiation | 3 specific reasons — draw from KB-07 |
| 10 | Getting started | Remove friction | Implementation timeline, support available |
| 11 | Pricing / next step | Close | [See pricing guidance] — one clear CTA |
| 12 | Q&A / Thank you | Closing | Contact details |

## Template 3 — Marketing Strategy / Campaign Deck

**Use when:** Presenting a quarterly plan, campaign proposal, or channel strategy to internal stakeholders  
**Slide count:** 12–18  
**Tone:** Strategic, evidence-based, action-oriented

| Slide # | Title | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Cover | Title, team, date |
| 2 | Objective | What we're trying to achieve — link to business goal |
| 3 | Audience | Who we're targeting — persona summary from KB-02 |

| 5 | Our positioning | How we're showing up — draw from KB-03 |
| 6 | Strategy | The overarching approach |
| 7 | Channel plan | Where we're showing up and why |

## Slide design principles (for FE Dev Agent context)

These apply to any Acefone-branded presentation:

**Layout rules:**
- One idea per slide — if you're writing a second point, make a second slide
- Titles are verbs or questions, not nouns — "We reduced churn by 40%" not "Churn reduction"
- Left-align body text — only centre titles
- Use white space — cramped slides lose the room

**Data visualisation:**
- Bar charts for comparisons
- Line charts for trends over time
- Pie charts only if the proportions are the story (and only 2–4 segments)
- Always label data points directly — no legend lookup required
- Source every chart — footnote the data source
