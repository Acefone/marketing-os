## Character count rules by channel

*The Paid Media Agent enforces these automatically. Listed here for human reference.*

### Google Search Ads
- Headline 1–3: 30 characters max each
- Description 1–2: 90 characters max each
- Display URL path: 15 characters max each

### LinkedIn Ads
- Intro text: 150 characters max (preview); 600 characters total
- Headline: 70 characters max
- Description (Sponsored Content): 255 characters

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
- Primary text: 125 characters for preview (2,200 total)
- Headline: 40 characters
- Description: 30 characters

### Email subject lines
- Preview text: 40–90 characters (for mobile — what shows in inbox)
- Avoid: ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, spam trigger words

## Tone guidelines — paid channels vs organic

| Element | Organic content | Paid copy |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| Length | Long-form, developed | Short, punchy — every word earns its place |
| Specificity | Evidence-led, cited | Lead with the benefit — save the proof for landing page |
| Urgency | Low — build over time | Moderate — create relevance, not panic |
| Product mention | Subtle (TOFU) to explicit (BOFU) | Present from first word in BOFU ads |
| Tone | Knowledgeable peer | Direct, benefit-forward, no fluff |

## Do-not-use phrases (paid copy specific)

*In addition to the banned vocabulary in KB-01, these are specifically off-limits in ad copy:*

| Phrase | Why |
|--------|-----|
| "Click here" | Generic — use a specific action instead |
| "Learn more" | Too soft for paid — replace with the specific action |
| "Best in class" | Unverifiable superlative |
| "Revolutionary" | Overused, not credible |
| "Don't miss out" | Manufactured urgency |
| "Limited time" | Only use if there's a real deadline — fake urgency damages trust |
| "Free" in headline without qualifying it | Can trigger Google policy flags |
