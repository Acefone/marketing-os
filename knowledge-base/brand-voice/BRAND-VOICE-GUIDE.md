## 4. Sentence and paragraph rules

**Sentence length:** [FILL IN — e.g. "Mix of short punchy sentences (8–12 words) and medium-length sentences (18–25 words). No sentence over 35 words."]

**Paragraph length:** [FILL IN — e.g. "3–4 sentences max per paragraph in blogs. 1–2 sentences for LinkedIn posts."]

**Active vs passive voice:** Always active in headlines and subheadings. Active preferred in body copy. Passive only when the actor is genuinely unknown.

**Numbers:** Write numerals for anything 10 and above. Write out one through nine. Always use % not "percent" in data-led content. Always include the unit (e.g. "saves 3 hours per week" not "saves 3 hours").

**Contractions:** [FILL IN — e.g. "Use contractions freely in blog and social content. Avoid in formal documents and sales enablement."]

## 5. Headline and subheading rules

**Headline formula options:**
- How to [achieve outcome] without [common pain]
- [Number] ways [persona] [solve/achieve/stop X]
- Why [common belief] is wrong — and what to do instead
- The [persona]'s guide to [topic]
- [Specific outcome] in [timeframe]: how [company type] did it

**What our headlines always do:**
- Lead with the reader's benefit or problem, not Acefone
- Use specific numbers over vague descriptors
- Promise the piece's specific value in the headline — not a clever riddle

**What our headlines never do:**
- Ask a question with an obvious yes/no answer
- Use exclamation marks
- Start with "Why you need to..." 
- Use superlatives without proof ("the best", "the only")

- Subheadings every 250–350 words

## 9. Editing quick-reference card

*The Editor Agent uses this to check drafts. Print it and keep it at your desk.*

| Check | Pass | Fail |
|-------|------|------|
| Tone | Confident and direct | Hedge-y ("may", "could potentially") |
| Vocabulary | Specific, plain English | Jargon, buzzwords, banned words |
| Headlines | Benefit or problem-led | Clever but unclear |
| Evidence | Specific numbers and sources | "Many businesses", "studies show" |
| CTA | One clear next step | Zero CTAs or more than one |
| Length | Exactly what's needed | Padded to hit word count |
| Voice | Reads like Acefone | Could be any SaaS company |
